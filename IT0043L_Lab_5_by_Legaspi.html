SDG 2: Zero Hunger

Why Zero Hunger is Essential?

SDG 2: Zero Hunger aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. Despite the world producing enough food to feed everyone, over 700 million people still suffer from hunger, and malnutrition remains a pressing issue, especially among children. This goal focuses not only on eradicating hunger but also on transforming food systems to become inclusive, sustainable, and resilient.

To meet this goal, there must be investments in rural infrastructure, agricultural research, and technology that supports small-scale farmers, especially women, indigenous peoples, and family farmers. Climate-smart agricultural practices, fair trade policies, and efficient food distribution are also essential. Actions to Support Zero Hunger

  • Buy from local farmers and food cooperatives
  • Donate food and funds to local pantries
  • Reduce personal and household food waste
  • Support education programs that teach about nutrition and sustainability
